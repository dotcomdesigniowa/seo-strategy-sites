Overview & Strategy Approach

This document outlines a comprehensive, data-driven SEO strategy designed specifically for Texas Building Center. The primary goal is to increase online visibility, drive qualified traffic, and generate consistent leads across the Texas Hill Country for barndominiums, custom homes, and metal buildings. The strategy is built on a foundation of high-intent, commercially relevant keywords mapped to high-value geographic markets; rather than targeting a broad, unfocused audience, this plan surgically places Texas Building Center in front of property owners and land buyers who are actively searching for barndominium builders, custom home contractors, and metal building companies right now. The barndominium and barndo search categories represent a dominant and rapidly growing segment of the Texas building market, and this strategy places heavy emphasis on capturing that demand. Every keyword combination was selected based on national search volume data, commercial intent signals, and strategic market opportunity across the Texas Hill Country, with the result being a plan that maximizes the value of every dollar invested.

Market Analysis & Tiering

We analyzed all 29 cities and communities confirmed in Texas Building Center's service area and evaluated each market based on population size, proximity to the Burnet headquarters, and overall search opportunity. The analysis produced three tiers of market priority. Tier 1 markets are the highest-population communities within the service area and form the backbone of the strategy. Marble Falls is the largest city in the service area and serves as the primary population center for the Lake LBJ corridor. Lampasas and Kingsland are strong county-seat and lake-community markets with high barndominium and custom home demand. Burnet anchors the strategy as the headquarters city with maximum local relevance. Tier 2 markets are strong secondary communities ideal for plan expansion. Tier 3 markets are smaller Hill Country communities that become viable targets as domain authority grows over time.
